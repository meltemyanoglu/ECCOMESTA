
\subsection{Functional Requirements Satisfaction Check}
The database must store:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The details of the \textbf{Customer}, including the following attributes: name and surname, e-mail, password
  \item  The details of the \textbf{Product}, including the following attributes: Price, Product ID, UPC, VAT, name
  
  \item The details of the \textbf{Employee}, such as employee id, name, date, surname, hire date, salary, etc.
  
  \item The details of the \textbf{Receipt}, including the supplier details, date, payment method, total amount and one product.
  
  \item The details of the supplier's supplies to, such as reorder level, stored in, stock quantity, etc.
  \item The details of the \textbf{Supplier} contract details, such as start-end dates, discount info, promotion order quantity
 \end{itemize}
 
 \paragraph{}
The system must allow:
  \begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Customers to login and signup.}
  
  \item \textbf{Stock Management }
  Store owners to input stock quantities for products, to track reorder levels for products and to transfer stock from one store to another
  
  \item \textbf{Purchase and Payment Management}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item  Customers to purchase products at stores
     \item Store owners to input purchase prices for products
       \item Customers to use their loyalty points while making a purchase
         \item Customers to view and download their receipts
          \item Store owners to record the payment methods used by customers
  \end{itemize}
 
  \item\textbf{Receipt and Transaction Management}
     \begin{itemize}
        \item   Store owners to generate and record unique receipt numbers for each purchase
         \item Customers to see transaction details, such as purchase quantity, date, and store location

    \end{itemize}
   
  \item\textbf{Product Information Management}
   \begin{itemize}
     \item   Store owners to input detailed product information, such as product ID, name, UPC, VAT, and price
         \item Store owners to track the location of each product within their store
           \item Store owners to manage product availability, including in-stock and out-of-stock statuses

    \end{itemize}

   \item\textbf{Store Information Management }
   \begin{itemize}
     \item  Store owners to input detailed store information, such as store ID, name, location, schedule, and manager details
         \item Employees to track product stock levels within their store
           \item Store owners to manage product supply chains, including supplier information and transfer schedules

    \end{itemize}
    

    \item\textbf{Category and Supplier Management }
     \begin{itemize}
     \item  Employees or managers to input detailed category information, such as category ID and name
         \item Store owners to input detailed supplier information, such as supplier ID, name, contact information, and contract details
           \item Customers to associate products with their respective categories and products

    \end{itemize}
   



     \item\textbf{Promotion and Discount Management }
      \begin{itemize}
     \item  Store owners to input detailed promotion and discount information, such as discount type, percentage, or amount
         \item Store owners to associate specific promotions and discounts with individual products

    \end{itemize}



\item\textbf{Reporting and Analysis}
Store owners to generate various types of reports, such as sales reports, inventory reports, and employee performance reports
and also to analyze data from these reports to identify trends and make informed business decisions.


\item\textbf{Security and User Management}
 \begin{itemize}
     \item   Store owners to create and manage user accounts for employees, suppliers, and customers
         \item Store owners to assign appropriate user roles and permissions to each user account individual products
         \item Store owners to monitor and control user access to sensitive data and functions within the system

    \end{itemize}
   


  
\end{itemize}
