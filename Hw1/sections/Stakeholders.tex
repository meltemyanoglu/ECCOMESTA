\section{Users and Stakeholders of the System}
%List of the users and stakeholders of your system: 
%who will use the system? what will their role be? %what kind of data they will manage?
From the database point of view users and stakeholders of the system can be listed as below:
\begin{itemize}
   
    \item  Store Managers and supervisors: A database system has been used by managers and supervisors to manage and monitor sales, manage stuffs, employee performance, and profitability.

    \item  Store Employees (Clerks, Cashiers, Stockers): all the sales process and customer requirements carried out by the employees 
    
    \item  Inventory Control Specialists: the individuals who are supposed to manage the items by using the barcodes to know whether it is expired or not, what is already sold out, and what needs to be revised based on customer interest

    \item IT Specialists and Database Administrators: They are very important for the database system since they are the ones who store, maintain, and secure all of the information. Also, they manage all the database environment
      
    \item Customers: a good system can have a significant impact on bringing the new customers and keeping the existing ones. As and end user, customers can define the whole system and the progress of the store 
 
    \end{itemize}
